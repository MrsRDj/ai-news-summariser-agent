HEADLINE: Nous Research Introduces Token Superposition Training, Accelerating LLM Pre-Training by 2.5x

SUMMARY: Nous Research has unveiled Token Superposition Training (TST), a novel two-phase pre-training strategy that enhances the efficiency of large language models (LLMs) by reducing training time by up to 2.5 times while maintaining the same FLOPs. This method involves averaging contiguous token embeddings in the first phase and reverting to standard prediction in the second, all without altering the model's architecture or inference behavior. The technique has been validated across a range of model sizes from 270M to 10B parameters.

KEY POINTS:
- TST reduces wall-clock training time by up to 2.5x while keeping FLOPs constant.
- The method employs a two-phase approach: the first phase averages token embeddings, and the second returns to standard next-token prediction.
- It is applicable to a broad range of model sizes, including 270M, 600M, 3B dense, and 10B-A1B MoE scales.
- No changes are required to the model architecture, tokenizer, optimizer, or inference-time behavior.

IMPACT: The introduction of TST has significant implications for AI developers and businesses by drastically reducing training times for large language models, enabling faster iteration and deployment in AI applications.
