SUMMARY: Deploying machine learning models into production poses significant risks, as offline evaluations may not reflect real-world complexities. This article outlines four strategies—A/B testing, Canary releases, Interleaved testing, and Shadow testing—that help mitigate these risks and ensure smoother transitions when introducing new models.

KEY POINTS:
- Directly replacing production models with new ones can lead to unforeseen complications.
- A/B testing allows for comparative analysis between the old and new models.
- Canary releases gradually roll out the new model to a small user base to monitor performance before full deployment.
- Interleaved testing evaluates model performance by alternating between old and new models during user interaction.
- Shadow testing runs the new model in the background to assess its performance without impacting users.

IMPACT: These strategies are crucial for businesses to minimize risks associated with model deployment, ensuring stability and reliability in machine learning applications.
